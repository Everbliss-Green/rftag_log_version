RFTag Release Notes

1.1 June 22, 2026

New features

  • Point of Interest (POI) pings — ping a location to the whole group; it drops a map pin and points everyone's LED ring toward the spot.
  • POI card in chat — pings appear as a tappable chat card with the coordinates and a shortcut to open the radar.
  • Off-screen icons stay on the edge — when a member or target is outside the visible map, its icon pins to the edge of the radar as an arrow pointing toward it. Tap the arrow to recenter the map on it.
  • Fall Detection Alarm toggle — turn the automatic alarm on or off from the profile screen. Turning it off asks to confirm, and the change is sent to your tag right away.
  • GPS source indicator — your “you are here” marker now shows whether your position is coming from your RFTag's GPS or from your phone's GPS, so you always know which one is in use.

Firmware (v2.2.2-85)

  • POI “ping a location” mode — the LED ring can now point at a fixed coordinate set by a leader; the ping is temporary and auto-clears after 5 minutes.
  • Fall detection on/off — the tag now honors the app's Fall Detection toggle. The manual SOS button still works regardless of the setting.
  • Distinct emergency lights — your own emergency now shows a slow red “breathe,” making it easy to tell apart from a received alert from someone else in the group.
  • Accurate no-fix reporting — when the tag loses its GNSS fix it now reports NO_FIX correctly instead of showing a stale position, which powers the new GPS source indicator.
  • In-app PIN security — BLE access is gated behind an in-app PIN (the value lasered on the device) instead of OS-level pairing.
  • Over-the-air BLE updates — firmware can update over BLE for easier recovery and updates; images are still verified before they're applied. The app will prompt you to update.
