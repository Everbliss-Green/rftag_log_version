What's new in 1.1

Point of Interest (POI) pings

Leaders can now ping a location to the whole group over LoRa and chat. The ping drops a map pin and points every member's LED ring toward the spot — so the whole group heads the same way.

When a target is off-screen, a colored arrow pins to the edge of the radar pointing toward it. Tap the arrow to recenter the map on that location.

iOS Android Needs firmware update

New POI card in chat

POI pings now appear as a friendly, tappable card in the group chat. It shows the pinged coordinates, a “Tap to open radar” shortcut, and a clear note that the orange LED ring is pointing toward that location.

iOS Android

Customizable device icon

Personalize how your tag shows up on the device card. Tap the device image to pick a color — orange, red, green, yellow, or black. Green is the new out-of-the-box default.

iOS Android

Fall Detection Alarm toggle

You can now turn the automatic fall-detection alarm on or off right from the profile screen, instead of it always running. The control appears below the device card when a tag is paired.

Turning it off asks for confirmation as a safety step, and the change is written to your wearable immediately.

iOS Android

Smarter “you are here” marker

Your own marker now reflects your tag's GPS status: it shows a phone-GPS badge when the tag has no fix, and greys out at your last known spot when there's no location at all — so you always know how accurate your position is.

iOS Android
Wearable firmware

New firmware bundled with 1.1

This release ships updated, signed wearable firmware that powers the new features:

  • POI LED-ring targeting — the ring points toward a pinged location.
  • Clearer light distinction between your own tag and received signals.
  • Over-the-air BLE updates (DFU) without an unlock gate, for smoother future updates.

The app will prompt you to update your wearable. Keep your tag nearby and charged while the update runs.
